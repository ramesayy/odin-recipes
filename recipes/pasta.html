Pasta

Description

Carbonara is an Italian pasta dish from Rome made with eggs, hard cheese, cured pork, and black pepper. The dish arrived at its modern form, with its current name, in the middle of the 20th century. The cheese is usually Pecorino Romano, Parmigiano-Reggiano, or a combination of the two. Spaghetti is the most common pasta, but fettuccine, rigatoni, linguine, or bucatini are also used. Normally guanciale or pancetta are used for the meat component, but lardons of smoked bacon are a common substitute outside Italy.

Ingredients

  • Eggs
  • Pasta of choice
  • Cheese
  • Olive oil
  • Guanciale/Pancetta
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Cook the pasta
  2. Mix the cheese with 2-3 ggs
  3. Cook the guanciale/pancetta at medium heat
  4. Put everything over the pasta in a different pan plus some pasta water
